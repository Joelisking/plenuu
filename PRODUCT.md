# Product

## Register

brand

> Plenuu has both a brand surface (the marketing site / landing pages, where design *is* the product and persuasion matters) and a product surface (the shopping experience on plenuu.com plus the Plenuu Business App for vendors). The default register is **brand**; override to `product` per task when working on the storefront, checkout, account, or vendor dashboard screens.

## Users

Two sides of a marketplace, spanning two markets (Ghana and the USA):

- **Shoppers** — everyday consumers in Ghana and the US buying fashion, electronics, home goods, beauty, food, and more. They arrive wanting selection, trust, and a clear sense that delivery will actually happen (3–5 days, tracked). Many pay with mobile money (MOMO) alongside cards. They are price-aware but not bargain-hunting; trust and reliability matter more than the lowest price.
- **Vendors** — independent sellers and small businesses who list and manage their stores entirely from the **Plenuu Business App** (mobile). Their job is to sign up, list products with photos/pricing/inventory, and reach customers across both markets. Zero listing fees to start; they want reach, a real-time sales view, and dependable fulfillment.

The marketing site's job is to convert both audiences: get shoppers to start browsing, and get vendors to download the Business App and list their first product.

## Product Purpose

Plenuu is a cross-border, multi-vendor e-commerce marketplace connecting Ghana and the USA, with delivery across Africa. It gives independent vendors a single place to sell to both markets, and gives shoppers one trusted storefront for everything from fashion to electronics to food. Success looks like: shoppers who trust Plenuu enough to make it their default marketplace, and vendors who can run a real cross-border business from their phone.

"International reach with local feel" is the core promise: global selection and shipping, but grounded in local payment methods, local currency (GH₵), and local delivery.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, modern, and eye-catching. Confident without shouting. The voice is direct and trustworthy, never hype-y or discount-driven: it sells reliability and selection, not coupons. Three words: **trustworthy, modern, confident.**

The brand should feel like a credible cross-border platform a vendor would stake their business on and a shopper would hand their card to. The wordmark's clear blue is the brand anchor (see Design Principles and the brand-color note); it carries the confident, modern feel without resorting to the premium-commerce cliché palette.

Emotional goals: confidence (this is real and reliable), clarity (I can find and trust what I need), momentum (it's easy to start, as a shopper or a vendor).

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — no purple-gradient hero, hero-metric template, or identical icon-card grids.
- **Cold corporate** — no stiff navy-and-gray enterprise feel. Plenuu serves real people across two cultures; it needs human warmth.
- **Cluttered / busy** — no dense listing-aggregator wall (think the crowded, overstuffed marketplace homepage). Curation and breathing room over cramming every product above the fold.
- **Cheap / discount** — no bargain-bin, loud-banner, coupon-site energy. Sales exist but trust leads; the brand must not undercut its own credibility.
- **The navy + gold "premium commerce" palette** — the original Figma mock leaned on deep navy plus gold/amber accents. That combination now reads as the AI-default look for commerce/marketplace and fintech sites (a reflex-reject lane). We are deliberately moving off it and anchoring on the wordmark blue instead.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the product.** Every screen should earn confidence: clear delivery promises, verified vendors, secure/local payment, honest pricing. Design choices that increase perceived reliability win over choices that merely look impressive.
2. **Local feel, global reach.** Honor both markets concretely: GH₵ pricing, mobile money, Ghana + USA delivery language, culturally grounded imagery. "International reach with local feel" is a design constraint, not just a tagline.
3. **Curate, don't dump.** Lead with selected, well-presented products and clear departments. Restraint and rhythm beat density; the page should feel edited, not aggregated.
4. **One identity, two audiences.** Shopper and vendor journeys share one coherent brand, but each has a clear, distinct call to action. Never make a vendor hunt for "start selling" or a shopper wade through vendor messaging.
5. **Eye-catching through craft, not noise.** "Eye-catching" comes from strong typographic hierarchy, confident use of the brand blue, and considered photography, not from loud banners, gradients, or decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA** as a standing constraint:

- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast; large text ≥ 3:1; placeholder text held to the same 4.5:1 bar.
- Full keyboard operability for all interactive elements (search, filters, cart, forms, vendor flows).
- Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
- Semantic markup and meaningful link/button labels (verb + object), since the audience spans varied devices, connection speeds, and assistive tech across two markets.
- Don't rely on color alone to convey state (e.g. sale/deal indicators need text or icon, not just red).
